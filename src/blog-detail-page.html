In fact, according to the Stack Overflow Developer Survey 2023, Go is among the top 10 most loved programming languages, with developers praising its performance and simplicity.

What is Golang? An Overview

Go (also termed as Golang), developed by Google, refers to an open-source programming language. The developers use Go to build networking and cloud services, web applications, and related software types across a range of frameworks and operating systems. Primarily used to manage business logic, backend development, server-side functionalities, and database interactions, Go is popular for its simplicity and efficiency.

Golang is statically typed, which means the variable type is known during compile time and can’t be varied at runtime. Due to its fast startup time, capability to run without a virtual machine, and low runtime overhead, Go has become a highly adopted language to write microservices. Additionally, the language is used to perform concurrent programming—a method to execute multiple tasks simultaneously, in partial order, or even out of order.

Inspired by the simplicity and productivity of Python, Go utilizes lightweight and concurrent functions called Goroutines, along with a collection of packages, to facilitate efficient dependency management. Designed to resolve multiple problems, such as build time issues, uncontrolled dependencies, complexities in writing automatic tools, effort duplication, and cross-language development concerns, Go is great for developers.

In short, Go is ideal because:

  • It is easy to learn and an excellent choice for developers.
  • It incorporates built-in concurrency and has a robust standard library.
  • It has a vast ecosystem of communities, partners, and tools.
History of Golang- How the Language Evolved

Google created Go in 2007 to address its software engineering challenges and provide a superior alternative to the C++ programming language. It required a language with excellent productivity levels, as multicore processors were highly popular at the time.

Go developers sought a programming language that was easy to use yet also possessed characteristics found in other languages, such as Python, C++, and JavaScript. Announced in 2009, Go (Golang) launched as an open-source project that enabled community members to collaborate on code and ideas. It also released version 1.0 in 2012.

After many years, new features and updates were added, like generics in 2022. The language’s popularity surged with its widespread adoption among large companies and multinational corporations, including Google, Dropbox, Cloudflare, MongoDB, Netflix, and Uber.
