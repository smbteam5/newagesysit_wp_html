NewAgeSysIT partnered with the client to develop a mobile-first clinical reference tool authored by a specialist Wound, Ostomy, and Continence Nurse Practitioner (WOC NP) with advanced hospice and palliative certification.

Augmenting Clinicians With Access to Wound Care Expertise

The client wanted to centralize the treatment guidance, wound and ostomy treatment formularies, and case studies into a unified mobile application. Our app development experts built a custom mobile app to support clinicians treating patients with wounds and ostomies, including those working in hospice, palliative, and general wound care settings.

Solution That Served Clinicians With Reliable
Clinical Content

Wound Care Pro delivers a specialized digital platform that modernizes clinicians' access to wound and ostomy care guidance, replacing fragmented references with a unified, mobile-first tool backed by WOC NP expertise. By combining structured clinical content, enterprise user management, and flexible billing, the application enhances the consistency of care, clinicians' confidence, and the scalability of wound care education across healthcare organizations. Our Project Challenges

Lack of Unified Management 

Healthcare organizations lack a unified way to provision, manage, and update wound care reference tools for multiple clinicians across sites.

Varying Quality in Patient Care

Clinicians treating complex wounds often rely on fragmented resources, leading to inconsistent treatment approaches and variability in patient care quality.

Limited Access to Expertise

Access to specialized wound and Ostomy expertise at the bedside or in the field is limited, especially for teams without on-site WOC specialists.

Time Constraints

Keeping staff updated with current treatment guidelines, formularies, and ostomy practices is time-consuming without a centralized digital resource.
